# Ski Edge Tuning Guide

## Why Edge Tuning Matters

Ski edges are made of hardened steel and are the primary contact point between the ski and the snow on turns. Sharp, correctly beveled edges grip the snow surface, allowing clean carving and confident edge hold on hardpack and ice. Dull, burred, or incorrectly beveled edges:

- Slip or chatter instead of holding a clean carve
- Catch unpredictably on hardpack or ice
- Require more physical effort to initiate turns
- Are more susceptible to rust

Regular edge maintenance is especially important for skiers who ski hardpack, groomed runs, or icy conditions frequently.

---

## Understanding Edge Bevel Angles

Edges are tuned with two distinct bevel angles:

### Base Bevel (Ramp Angle)
The angle ground into the bottom of the ski at the edge, measured from flat. This lifts the edge slightly off the snow.

- **Standard range:** 0.5° to 1°
- **Lower bevel (0.5°):** More edge engagement, better carving. Good for aggressive skiers on groomed runs.
- **Higher bevel (1°+):** Forgives catching edges. Better for beginners, powder, and bumps.
- **Recreational default:** 1° is the most common shop tune for all-mountain skis.

### Side Bevel (Edge Angle)
The angle filed into the side of the edge, making it sharper than a 90° square.

- **Standard range:** 2° to 4°
- **2°:** Standard recreational tune. Balanced grip and forgiveness.
- **3°:** More aggressive grip on hardpack. Good for advanced skiers.
- **4°+:** Racing or expert use only. Very aggressive — catches easily in soft snow.
- **Recreational default:** 2–3° for all-mountain, 2° for beginners and powder skiers.

**Combined notation:** A tune labeled "1°/3°" means 1° base bevel, 3° side bevel.

## Tools

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Bastard mill file (8–10") | Removes burrs and material; aggressive cut |
| Diamond stone (100, 200, 400 grit) | Sharpening and polishing; finer than file |
| Gummy stone | Deburring, rust removal, light touch-up |
| Edge tuning guide / file guide | Holds file or stone at the correct bevel angle |
| Side edge guide | Sets side bevel angle for filing |
| Base edge guide | Sets base bevel angle |
| Deburring tool / ceramic stick | Final deburr after filing |
| Rust eraser / fibertex | Light rust removal |
| Vise or ski stand | Holds ski stable |

## When to Tune Edges

**Sharp enough:** Edges should feel sharp when you carefully drag your fingernail lightly across the side edge. A sharp edge will shave a thin layer off your nail. This is the standard "thumbnail test."

**Time to tune:**
- Skis feel like they are sliding sideways instead of gripping on hardpack
- Visible burrs or nicks when you sight down the edge
- Rust spots along the edge (rust weakens steel and accelerates burring)
- It has been 8–12+ ski days since last sharpening (varies by conditions — icy runs dull edges faster)
- After a rock strike (single aggressive deburring pass often sufficient for minor strikes)

## Step-by-Step Edge Tuning

### Step 1 — Inspect
Place the ski base-up in a vise. Use a marker to color the side edge along the length of the ski. This helps you see where your file is removing material — wherever the marker is removed after filing, you are making contact.

### Step 2 — Side Edge Filing
1. Set your side edge guide to the target angle (e.g., 2° or 3°).
2. Clamp or hold a bastard mill file in the guide.
3. Place the guide against the edge and push the file tip-to-tail in one smooth, consistent stroke. Never pull (pulling the file reduces its effectiveness and risks gouging).
4. Apply moderate, even pressure. Let the file do the work — do not press hard.
5. After several passes (check with marker — all marker should be removed), switch to a diamond stone (100 grit, then 200 grit) in the same guide. This refines the edge left by the file.
6. Finish with 400-grit diamond stone for a polished edge.

### Step 3 — Base Edge
Base bevel tuning requires a base edge guide (not the same as side edge guide). This is typically a set-and-forget operation for recreational skiers — once your base bevel is set, you only need to maintain the side edge during regular tuning.

If your base bevel needs adjustment (new skis, or after base grind):
1. Set the base edge guide to 0.5° or 1°.
2. Run diamond stones (not file) along the base edge tip-to-tail.
3. Remove only what is needed — base bevel changes are permanent until the next base grind.

### Step 4 — Deburr
After filing and stoning, the edge will have a fine wire burr on the opposite side. Remove it:
1. Run a gummy stone or ceramic deburring stick lightly along the base edge (the bottom of the ski) at 90° — just a few passes.
2. Run lightly along the side edge as well.
3. The thumbnail test should now feel sharp and clean, not grabby.

### Step 5 — Detune Tips and Tails
The edge at the very tip and tail of the ski (the last 5–10cm before the curve) should be slightly duller than the rest of the edge. Sharp tip/tail edges catch too aggressively in the snow on flat terrain and can be dangerous.

1. Take a gummy stone or 400-grit diamond stone.
2. Lightly run it at 90° along the last 5–10cm of edge at the tip and tail — 3–5 passes.
3. The edges should feel slightly less sharp here than the midsection.

### Step 6 — Rust Treatment
If you see surface rust (red-orange discoloration on the edge):
1. Remove rust with a gummy stone — press firmly and rub back and forth along the rusted area.
2. Follow with light diamond stone passes to restore edge quality.
3. Apply a thin coat of wax over the edges during waxing — this is your best rust prevention.

## Racing Tune vs. Recreational Tune

| Aspect | Recreational | Racing |
|---|---|---|
| Side bevel | 2–3° | 3–5° |
| Base bevel | 1° | 0–0.5° |
| Tip/tail detune | Yes (5–10cm) | Minimal or none |
| Polish level | 200-grit finish | 400-grit or higher |
| Frequency | Every 8–12 ski days | After every 1–3 race days |

## When It's Beyond DIY

Take skis to a shop for:
- **Bent edges:** An edge that has bent outward from an impact cannot be filed flat — it needs to be re-pressed by a shop.
- **Edge separation:** If the edge has pulled away from the base (you can feel a gap), shop epoxy re-bond is required.
- **Base grind needed:** If the base has deep gouges, significant core shots, or is visibly convex/concave, a shop base grind (stone grind) is required before proper edge tuning is possible. Filing edges on a wavy base produces uneven results.
- **Broken edge section:** A cracked or missing section of edge requires replacement — shop only.

---

## Rust Prevention

- Always dry edges after each ski day. Water left on steel edges will rust overnight.
- Apply wax over edges during your regular wax job — this creates a light barrier.
- Store skis in a dry environment. Do not leave wet skis in a ski bag for extended periods.
- Apply a light coat of universal wax to edges (over-iron slightly past the edge, then scrape the base) before long-term storage.
